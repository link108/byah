Good internal notes are not polished. They are accurate enough to trust, small enough to
scan quickly, and blunt about the tradeoffs that mattered.

## What survives

The notes worth keeping usually answer three questions:

1. What problem showed up?
2. What changed?
3. Why was that tradeoff acceptable at the time?

If those answers are written down clearly, future-you can recover the shape of the
decision without reconstructing the entire week.
